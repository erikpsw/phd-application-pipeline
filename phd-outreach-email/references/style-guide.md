# PhD Outreach Email Style Guide

## Target Style

Write like a capable applicant, not marketing copy.

- Verify the professor's current work before drafting.
- Paraphrase one concrete research detail instead of listing paper titles.
- State the target intake clearly.
- Use concrete technical nouns and short paragraphs.
- Keep the first email around 170–230 words.
- Use no more than two applicant evidence points.
- Keep future jobs and planned projects in future tense.
- Avoid em dashes and exaggerated praise.
- Use phrases such as `aligns with`, `connects to`, or `is relevant to`.

## Subject Patterns

- `PhD Inquiry: [Specific Direction] ([Target Intake])`
- `PhD Inquiry: [Method] for [Application Area] ([Target Intake])`
- `Prospective PhD Student: [Research Area] ([Target Intake])`

## Opening

Use:

```text
My name is [APPLICANT_NAME], and I am [DEGREE_OR_CURRENT_STATUS].
I am writing to inquire about PhD opportunities for [TARGET_INTAKE].
Your research on [VERIFIED_ADVISOR_DETAIL] aligns with the direction I hope to pursue.
```

Avoid long biographies in the opening.

## Background Paragraph

Select one primary evidence point and, at most, one supporting point:

```text
My recent research on [PROJECT_OR_PUBLICATION] focused on [METHOD] for [PROBLEM].
This connects to your work on [ADVISOR_METHOD_OR_PROBLEM], particularly [SPECIFIC_CHALLENGE].
```

For a planned role:

```text
I will soon join [ORGANIZATION], where I expect to gain exposure to [RELEVANT_AREA].
```

Never write `I currently work` when the role has not started.

## Research Interest

Use one forward-looking sentence:

```text
Looking ahead, I am interested in how [METHOD] can support [PROBLEM],
especially in settings where [CONSTRAINT].
```

## Closing

```text
I have attached my CV and would appreciate knowing whether you anticipate
taking PhD students for [TARGET_INTAKE].

Thank you for your time.

Best regards,
[APPLICANT_NAME]
[APPLICANT_EMAIL]
```

## Avoid

- `groundbreaking`
- `world-class`
- `prestigious`
- `fascinating`
- `incredible`
- `transformative`
- `deeply inspired`
- `perfect fit`

Replace vague claims with specific evidence and a verified research connection.
